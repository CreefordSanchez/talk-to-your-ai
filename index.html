The Lost Compass of Aradien Beneath the golden rays of a waning sun, the bustling harbor of Aradien thrived with energy. Merchants barked about exotic wares, sailors sang shanties, and the air carried the scent of salt and adventure. In the heart of it all stood Kael, a young mapmaker with a shock of unruly brown hair and an insatiable curiosity about the world beyond his charts. Kael’s life had been ordinary—until the day a cloaked stranger stumbled into his shop, clutching a compass with trembling hands. “This… must not fall into the wrong hands,” the stranger rasped, his voice thin as parchment. “Protect it, and you may find what you seek.” Before Kael could ask what that meant, the stranger vanished into the crowd, leaving only the peculiar compass behind. It was unlike any Kael had seen, its glass face etched with faint, glowing runes that shifted in patterns too intricate to follow. Chapter 1: The Call to Adventure That night, as Kael studied the compass, its needle began to spin wildly, then stopped, pointing toward a place that didn’t exist on any of his maps. Curious and unnerved, he decided to follow its direction. The next morning, Kael packed his things—a journal, rations, and his favorite pen—then boarded the first ship heading toward the compass's bearing. The ship, The Stormchaser, was helmed by Captain Lyra, a fierce yet kind-hearted sailor with a reputation for chasing impossible legends. Kael shared the compass and his story with her, and to his relief, she didn’t laugh. “A compass that points to the unknown?” Lyra mused, her green eyes gleaming. “Sounds like just the kind of trouble I’m looking for.
